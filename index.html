How full is each CX team's day?

For every sales area, this shows the work one Customer Experience (CX) team member handles in an average day — case handling, outbound calls, inbound calls, and production tasks — and how much of their shift it fills.

Snapshot as of 25 June 2026 · figures cover a 61 working-day period

How to read this

Each bar is one person's average working day in that region. The coloured parts show what fills the day; the empty part is time left in the shift.

The percentage is how full the day is. At 100% the day is completely full. Over 100% (red) means there is more work than there are hours in the shift — the team is stretched and likely needs help.

How each bar is built: for each work type, total time = volume × average time. The four are added up, divided by working days, then divided by the number of people — giving the hours one person handles per day. That is compared to their shift to get the percentage. The headline is in hours; the split beneath each bar shows minutes per person per day for each activity.
Assumptions & notes: Production tasks are estimated at 45 seconds each (an average, not measured per task — change it in the panel if you have a firmer number). Call times are average minutes per call; Frabel and UKI outbound were reported in seconds and converted. SEA and Turkey had no call activity, shown as zero. Turkey (·) has tiny case volume, so its per-person figure isn't meaningful. All volumes are assumed to cover the same period as the case data. Working days are set per region (default 61) since local holidays differ — use the box at the top to set them all at once, or override any single region in the panel.
